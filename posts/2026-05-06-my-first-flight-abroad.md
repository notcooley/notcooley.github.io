## Good evening Levi, 

Or good morning. I’m unsure what time it is there, let alone when this will hit the airways. I’m writing the first part of this piece right before I begin boarding my first international flight. Emily and my excitement only hindered slightly by the beers we spent too much money on near our terminal in Atlanta.

I guess I’m also nervous. It will be nearly 9 hours on a plane - that’s a full work day! I’ll pick up in Copenhagen but right now I’m just excited to get there, and nervous too.

—

Just landed in Copenhagen. It was a pretty surreal experience. After an ok sleep, we were greeted by a weirdly quiet international terminal. Emily made it through customs with ease, while I took longer because their fingerprint reader didn’t work on me. I tried to explain that I was a rock climber. After customs we were thrown into a very busy airport indeed. It was pretty awesome to hear people speaking different languages all around me. While I’m sure a bulk of them were speaking Danish and what sounded like a handful of Germans, to a naive American, it felt like new sounds everywhere.

I’ve been told that jet lag is brutal, but at the moment I don’t feel it. Or at least I don’t think I feel it. I’ve never been on a flight that long, nor a time difference so large. 

I find it fascinating how similar the outside of the airport is compared to those in America. I figured it would be standardized but you could drop this place in Omaha and no one would bat an eye. The similarities end at the outer walls though, because the interiors contrast starkly. While airports back home would have IKEA style tiling and furniture, Copenhagen has concrete walls and more brutalist seating. Ironic given our exceptional proximity to IKEA’s homeland. Another interesting note is the low hanging ceilings, something I noticed the second I got here. I remember seeing a video about airport architecture  and they pointed out that many airports make the terminals more grand. Tall windows and high ceilings are used to accentuate the act of air travel. Meanwhile TSA should feel a bit more confined and secure. Here however, all the ceilings are quite low and the windows squinty, making it all feel smaller. 

I spent 144,00 Danish kroners on coffee and food for us. I find it easy to spend money when I don’t really know how much I’m spending. A trait I’m sure no few businesses appreciate arrogant Americans for. 

Our last flight boards soon, and after I leave the airport maybe I’ll actually feel like I’m in another country. 
—

We finally arrived in Greece! After landing on the tarmac and taking a bus back to the terminal, we were greeted with a smoking lounge right inside the airport. Emily got a kick out of this quickly asking if she should smoke cigarettes in Greece. Mind you this woman wants to throw up at the smell of one, but as soon as we saw the old German lady on the flight hurry in and pull out a long thin darty in the fish bowl that was the smoke lounge, Emily was on board. The same old lady managed to beat us to the baggage claim, talk about efficiency! 

That’s all for now, adventure awaits!
